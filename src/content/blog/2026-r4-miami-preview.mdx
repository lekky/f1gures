The 2026 season comes to [Miami](/circuits/miami/) on Sunday May 3 after a
five-week gap - the longest mid-season break in the calendar. Sprint weekend
format, Florida heat, and a Mercedes team that has won two on the bounce with
race-pace numbers nobody in the paddock can explain.

## The state of play

<DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> leads <DriverChip ref="russell" /> 68-55 in the
drivers' championship. Mercedes lead Ferrari 123-77 in the constructors'.
<DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> is third in the drivers' table on 42, ahead of
<DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> on 35. <DriverChip ref="piastri" />'s Suzuka P2
moved McLaren ahead of Red Bull and within striking range of Haas. Five-week
break means upgrades. Every team in the points-paying half of the grid is
expected to bring a meaningful package.

## What Miami asks of the cars

Long straights, tight chicane-style stadium section, low-grip surface that
takes the whole weekend to rubber in. The new active-aero rear wing should
matter more here than at any track so far - two DRS-equivalent straights
plus a third near-straight. Teams that have got the wing transitions clean
will find easy laptime. Teams that haven't will have a messy weekend.

## What to watch

- **<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />'s upgrade.** Red Bull have been
  trailing a major package for Miami all month. If it doesn't work, the
  season story stops being *Mercedes are dominant* and starts being *Red Bull
  are broken*. P8 in Suzuka was the floor; Marko's quotes suggest it can go
  lower.
- **McLaren's Sunday.** <DriverChip ref="piastri" />'s Suzuka P2 was the
  Australian's first sign that the car the team have been telling us about
  in private actually exists. A second strong weekend would make it a trend.
- **Sprint points.** With Mercedes' race pace looking unassailable, the
  smaller teams that pick up sprint points - one-stop strategy, cooler tyre
  temps, less rubber on the surface - have an outsized opportunity. Haas
  showed in Shanghai how to convert this.
- **Hamilton's first US race for Ferrari.** He's never won in Miami. The
  tifosi turn up for this one in numbers and his result tends to set the
  tone for the European leg of the season.

Sprint Saturday at 12:00 local, race Sunday at 16:00 local (20:00 UTC).
