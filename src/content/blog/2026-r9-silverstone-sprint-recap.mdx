Ferrari had the pole. Mercedes had the race. On a bright Saturday morning at
[Silverstone](/circuits/silverstone/), <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> lined up
first for the sprint on home soil - the storybook grid slot in front of a home
crowd that has wanted exactly this all season. Then the lights went out, and
<DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> took it away from him.

The championship leader started second, got ahead of the Ferrari, and drove
away. By the flag it was 2.745s and never in question. Nineteen laps of the
short sprint format, no safety cars, no drama at the front - just the
19-year-old confirming, again, that 2026 is his season and everyone else is
racing for the scraps.

<DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> had done the hard part on Friday, beating the
Mercedes to sprint pole in a Ferrari that has flattered to deceive all year.
But the launch was <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />'s, the lead followed inside
the opening laps, and from there the sprint became a demonstration. Eight
points, the maximum on offer, and a Saturday that told the paddock nothing it
did not already suspect.

The best drive belonged to <DriverChip ref="norris" />. He started sixth,
finished third, and was the only car in the top group to climb through it -
past both the Verstappen Red Bull and his own team-mate to steal the last step
of the podium. For a McLaren team that has spent a month watching Mercedes and
Ferrari trade the front rows, six points from a sprint they qualified sixth
for is the kind of Saturday that quietly matters.

Behind him <DriverChip ref="russell" /> came home fourth and
<DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> fifth, the sister cars shadowing their leaders
without the pace to trouble them. The weekend's quiet loser was
<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />, who lined up third and slid to sixth,
picked off rather than picking others off - the wrong direction on a track
where Red Bull needed a statement. <DriverChip ref="piastri" /> took seventh
from seventh, and <DriverChip ref="lawson" /> grabbed the final point in
eighth for the RB team.

No retirements, a clean 22-car finish, only <DriverChip ref="stroll" /> and
<DriverChip ref="perez" /> lapped at the back. A tidy sprint - and a tidy eight
points onto a lead that was already the story of the season.

The eight points push <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> to 179 and stretch his
cushion over <DriverChip ref="russell" /> back out to 43 - an all-Mercedes top
two, with <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> a further four back in third after his
sprint runner-up. The interesting shuffle is just behind: <DriverChip ref="norris" />'s
podium vaults him clear of the four-way midfield knot into fourth outright,
ahead of <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />, <DriverChip ref="piastri" /> and
<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />, who are now covered by seven points for
the last of the top places.

That is the whole 2026 season in one Saturday: a leader who converts every
opportunity, a Mercedes lock at the front, and a compressed fight for the
best-of-the-rest scraps behind them.

The [British Grand Prix](/races/2026/9/) itself runs later on Sunday, 5 July -
Hamilton starting from the front on home ground with a point to prove, and
Antonelli looking to turn a perfect Saturday into a perfect weekend.
