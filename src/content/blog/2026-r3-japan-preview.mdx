[Suzuka](/circuits/suzuka/) on Sunday March 29. The third round, the first
back-to-back-to-back-week gap in the calendar, and the first weekend the 2026
cars get asked the questions the regulation reset was supposed to answer:
does the active-aero wing work cleanly through 130R? Can the cars sustain
the high-speed downforce levels these regulations were designed for? Is the
Mercedes advantage circuit-agnostic or has it been flattered by two low-speed
tracks?

## The state of the championship

After Melbourne and Shanghai: <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> and <DriverChip ref="russell" />
tied on 43 points at the top, <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> and <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />
27 each, and <DriverChip ref="bearman" /> a quiet but extraordinary fifth on
16. Red Bull are fourth in constructors on 12 points - 8 from <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />'s
P20-to-P6 recovery in Melbourne, 4 from <DriverChip ref="hadjar" />'s P8 in
Shanghai. <DriverChip ref="norris" /> is still on zero after the Shanghai
hydraulic failure cost McLaren a result that should have been there for the
taking.

## Why Suzuka matters more than usual

The circuit's first sector is a sustained high-downforce test. S-curves into
Dunlop into Degner asks the front axle to plant the car at 220+ km/h for
fifteen seconds at a time. If the W17's race-pace advantage holds here, it
holds anywhere. If Ferrari close it - and Suzuka is exactly the kind of track
Ferrari historically over-performs at - the championship reopens.

## The other questions

- **Verstappen at home.** Honda's home race, the last one in the current PU
  partnership. The RB22 has looked like a midfield car in race trim for two
  rounds. A Suzuka recovery would mean very different things from a Melbourne
  recovery - the car would need to actually be competitive, not just driven
  brilliantly.
- **McLaren's recovery weekend.** Round three is the latest the [Woking team](/teams/mclaren/)
  has ever been pointless at the start of a season. <DriverChip ref="piastri" />'s
  start in Shanghai was a low point. Norris hasn't completed a race yet.
- **<DriverChip ref="hadjar" />.** Two strong qualifying performances ruined
  by car failures in races. Suzuka is where rookies traditionally either
  arrive or get found out.
- **The weather.** Forecasts on Wednesday show a 40% chance of rain on Sunday.
  A wet Suzuka with the new regulations - cars no driver has raced in the
  rain at speed yet, active-aero behaviour in standing water still untested -
  would be the first genuine unknown of 2026.

Lights out on Sunday March 29 at 14:00 local (05:00 UTC).
