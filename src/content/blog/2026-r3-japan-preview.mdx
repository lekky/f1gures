[Suzuka](/circuits/suzuka/) on Sunday March 29. The third round, the first
back-to-back-to-back-week gap in the calendar, and the first weekend the 2026
cars get asked the questions the regulation reset was supposed to answer:
does the active-aero wing work cleanly through 130R? Can the cars sustain
the high-speed downforce levels these regulations were designed for? Is the
Mercedes advantage circuit-agnostic or has it been flattered by two low-speed
tracks?

## Why Suzuka matters more than usual

The circuit's first sector is a sustained high-downforce test. S-curves into
Dunlop into Degner asks the front axle to plant the car at 220+ km/h for
fifteen seconds at a time. If the W17's race-pace advantage holds here, it
holds anywhere. If Ferrari close it - and Suzuka is exactly the kind of track
Ferrari historically over-performs at - the championship reopens.
