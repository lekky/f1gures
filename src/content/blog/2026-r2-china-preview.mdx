[Shanghai](/circuits/shanghai/) gets the season's first sprint format on March
13-15. One hour of practice on Friday morning, sprint qualifying that evening,
a 100km sprint Saturday afternoon, full qualifying Saturday evening, race
Sunday afternoon. Two race results banked in three days. It's a format that
rewards teams who turn up with a finished car and punishes teams still
unpicking what their car is.

## The Mercedes question

Melbourne wasn't a one-off. The W17 spent the entire race controlling pace at
the front, and the long-run gap to <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />'s Ferrari was
big enough that a different circuit, different temperature and different tyre
compound shouldn't bridge it. Shanghai's medium-temp asphalt and long
right-handers reward a car that's stable on entry and easy on the rear - both
W17 strengths in testing. If Mercedes are 1-2 here too, the title fight starts
to look one-sided very early.

## What to watch on Friday

The single-hour practice tells you which teams have brought upgrades that work
and which have brought ones that don't. Watch the long-run times late in the
session - that's the only honest race-pace data anyone gets before sprint
qualifying.

Sprint Saturday at 11:00 local, Grand Prix Sunday at 15:00 local (07:00 UTC).
