Five rounds in, four wins for <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />. He didn't have
the fastest car on Saturday and he didn't lead the first half of the race,
but he was the one who finished. [Montreal](/races/2026/5/) was the most
chaotic Sunday of 2026 - two pole-favourites out before half-distance,
McLaren's recovery weekend obliterated, a Ferrari second place and the first
Red Bull podium of the season - and the championship leader simply collected
what fell to him.

## The race

<DriverChip ref="russell" /> had the weekend in hand. He won the sprint on
Saturday, took pole on Saturday evening, and led the opening 29 laps of
Sunday's race from the front. Then the W17 stopped on the back straight
without warning - Mercedes have not yet released a cause - and the lead
passed to <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />, who had run second from lights out
and never looked under pressure thereafter. Fastest lap on the final tour
(1:14.210), 10.768s clear at the flag, his fourth win from five.

The race behind him reset twice. <DriverChip ref="norris" /> qualified third
and was running third when the McLaren went silent on lap 38 - the second
points-paying retirement of the day from a car that had been in contention
on merit. <DriverChip ref="piastri" /> started fourth and finished eleventh,
a lap down, having spent the second half of the race trapped behind
recovering midfielders he could not pass. Eight days after Miami suggested
McLaren had turned the corner, Montreal said wait.

<DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> took second from fifth on the grid - his best
result for Ferrari, and the team's first podium since his own in China.
<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> was third from sixth, Red Bull's first
visit to the rostrum in 2026 and the kind of measured drive the championship
leader of the last four seasons has built his career on. <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />
finished fourth from eighth, recovering from a poor Saturday but losing
out to his teammate for the second weekend running.

The story of the midfield was <DriverChip ref="hadjar" />, who finished
fifth from seventh on the grid. The other Red Bull, two months into his
second season, on the lead lap, ahead of both Ferraris on race pace through
the middle stint. The car has clearly moved forward, but Montreal is also
the first race where the other side of the Red Bull garage has answered the
question of whether anyone but Verstappen can deliver in it.

<DriverChip ref="colapinto" /> took sixth - Alpine's best result of the
year - and <DriverChip ref="lawson" /> seventh after starting twelfth.
<DriverChip ref="gasly" />, <DriverChip ref="sainz" /> and <DriverChip ref="bearman" />
filled out the points.

## The DNFs

- <DriverChip ref="albon" /> - lap 11, suspension after kerb contact at turn 9
- <DriverChip ref="alonso" /> - lap 23, Aston Martin power unit
- <DriverChip ref="russell" /> - lap 29, mechanical failure while leading
- <DriverChip ref="norris" /> - lap 38, McLaren stopped on the pit straight
- <DriverChip ref="perez" /> - lap 39, contact with the Wall of Champions

The Wall got one. The preview promised a hit list and it claimed one car
in 28 years of trying to claim a champion - fitting enough.

The gap from Antonelli to Russell is now 49 points - nearly two race wins -
and the second-place battle has compressed into a four-way tie within 22
points covering Russell, Hamilton, Leclerc and Norris. Red Bull have moved
into fourth in the constructors on the back of one weekend; the upgrade
worked, the question is whether they can keep it on the car at Barcelona.

[Monaco](/races/2026/6/) next Sunday. The first true low-speed circuit of
the year, the one where the 2026 active-aero rules have been quietly
flagged as a question mark since pre-season, and the place championship
leaders are historically allowed exactly one mistake.
