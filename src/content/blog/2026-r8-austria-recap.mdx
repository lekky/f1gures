The preview called Austria an audit of Red Bull's season. It turned into a
small act of defiance instead. On the circuit that carries their name,
<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> dragged a car that has been seventh-best
all year onto the second step of the podium - the loudest cheer of the
weekend, and the closest thing to a home win the hosts were going to get.

But the win itself was never in doubt. <DriverChip ref="russell" /> took pole
on Saturday, led [every lap that mattered](/races/2026/8/) on Sunday, and
crossed the line 1.6s clear for his second victory of 2026. Mercedes leave
Spielberg with another one of those weekends where the question isn't whether
they win, but which of their drivers does.

## The race

<DriverChip ref="russell" /> made it look routine. Pole, a clean launch, and a
lead he managed rather than defended for all 71 laps. It's his second win of
the year after Canada, and the second time in 2026 he has converted a Saturday
pole into a Sunday that the rest of the field only watched. Mercedes now have
six wins from eight.

The drama was behind him. <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> started fifth
and was second by the flag, picking off the cars ahead through Spielberg's
braking zones - exactly the corners the preview flagged as the lap's
decision-makers. It's only his second podium of the season, but doing it at
the Red Bull Ring, with the grandstands in orange and blue, is the result
the team needed to point to after a brutal run of Sundays.

<DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> completed the podium from fourth on the grid
and took fastest lap (1:10.374) for good measure. The championship leader
didn't have the pace to fight Russell and didn't need it - third place and
the bonus point stretch his advantage back out, and his seventh podium in
eight rounds is the kind of consistency that wins titles long before the maths
says so.

The race of the day belonged to <DriverChip ref="piastri" />, who climbed from
seventh to fourth and was the only car in the top group to gain meaningful
ground - a quietly important result for a McLaren team that has spent a month
watching others score. <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> went the other way,
sliding from third on the grid to fifth at the flag, and <DriverChip ref="hadjar" />
again outscored the narrative, sixth from eighth in the other Red Bull.
<DriverChip ref="norris" /> took seventh.

The weekend's worst story was <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />. He qualified
second, started alongside Russell with a real shot at the front row converting
to a podium, and finished eighth - 45 seconds adrift, beaten by both McLarens,
both Red Bulls and the sister Ferrari. Whatever happened to the car after
lights out, it turned the brightest Ferrari Saturday in weeks into the team's
flattest Sunday. <DriverChip ref="lawson" /> and rookie <DriverChip ref="arvid_lindblad" />
took the last two points for the RB team, both a lap down.

## The DNFs

- <DriverChip ref="bottas" /> - lap 2, Cadillac
- <DriverChip ref="perez" /> - lap 4, Cadillac
- <DriverChip ref="sainz" /> - lap 23, Williams
- <DriverChip ref="stroll" /> - lap 45, Aston Martin

Both Cadillacs were gone inside the first four laps - a miserable afternoon
for the season's newest team on one of the calendar's shortest races.

Russell's win lifts him past Hamilton into second, and the top of the table is
now an all-Mercedes affair - 171 to 131, a 40-point gap, with the rest of the
field more than 90 points off the lead. Behind the silver cars the midfield of
the championship has compressed into a knot: Piastri, Leclerc and Norris are
covered by a single point at 80-79-79, with Verstappen six further back. Four
drivers, four points, one of the few genuine fights 2026 has left to offer.

[Silverstone](/races/2026/9/) is next on 5 July - faster, longer, and a real
test of whether Spielberg was a Red Bull revival or just a good day at the
office.
