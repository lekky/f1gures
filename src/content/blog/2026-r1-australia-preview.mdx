The 2026 season starts at [Albert Park](/circuits/albert_park/) on Sunday March 8,
and for the first time in a decade nobody is really sure who's quick. The new
power-unit regulations - 50/50 split between the internal-combustion engine
and the electric motor, dropped MGU-H, mandated active-aero front and rear -
mean every single car on the grid is a clean-sheet design. Pre-season testing
in Bahrain ran for six days. The lap times told us almost nothing useful.

## What testing did suggest

Mercedes looked the most polished. The W17 racked up the most kilometres of
any car in Bahrain and the long-run pace - never the headline thing teams
actually optimise for in testing - put them comfortably ahead of [Ferrari](/teams/ferrari/)
and [McLaren](/teams/mclaren/) in race-trim simulations. Take that with the
usual grain of salt: fuel loads and engine modes are guarded secrets in
February, and a quick test can flatter a programme that's been built around it.

[Red Bull](/teams/red_bull/) looked uncharacteristically scrappy. <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />
finished the last day pointing out a list of correlation issues between the
simulator and the real car. Whether that's a serious problem or a four-time
champion managing expectations in public will become clear roughly forty
minutes into qualifying on Saturday.

## The storylines

- **<DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> in his second full season.** A year of
  Mercedes data under his belt, the same car as <DriverChip ref="russell" />,
  and a regulation reset that means his rookie-year deficit to the field is
  gone. If the W17 is the car the testing data hinted at, he has a real shot
  at his first pole.
- **<DriverChip ref="hamilton" />'s first race in red.** Twelve seasons at
  Mercedes, then Ferrari. Melbourne is a happy hunting ground for him -
  two wins, eight podiums - but a brand-new car is a brand-new car.
- **The active-aero rear wing.** The headline new feature: drivers can deploy
  a low-drag mode on straights without DRS-style activation zones. Expect a
  messy first race as the field works out which corners can be taken with
  the wing still flat.

Albert Park itself doesn't ask the new cars hard questions about high-speed
aero, which makes it a useful but incomplete first data point. The real
read-out on the pecking order comes at [Suzuka](/circuits/suzuka/) in three
weekends' time. For now, the only honest answer is: watch and see.

Lights out at 15:00 local on Sunday March 8 (04:00 UTC).
