The sprint recap ended with Antonelli looking to turn a perfect Saturday into
a perfect weekend. Silverstone had other ideas. <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />
took [pole](/races/2026/9/) by 0.175s on Saturday afternoon, set the fastest
lap of the race on Sunday - and finished fifteenth in a damaged car. Into the
vacuum stepped
<DriverChip ref="leclerc" />, who won the British Grand Prix by 0.427s from
<DriverChip ref="russell" /> in a finish that put the top four within 1.2
seconds of each other. His first victory of 2026, Ferrari's second in three
rounds, and the first real dent in the championship leader's season since
Barcelona.

## The race

The grid promised a fight: <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> on pole at 1:28.111,
<DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> alongside, <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> and
<DriverChip ref="russell" /> on row two. For 52 laps [Silverstone](/circuits/silverstone/)
delivered one - just not the one the polesitter was part of.

The polesitter's afternoon was over almost before it began. <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />
picked up damage that blunted the car, and with it went the places: the
championship leader slid out of the lead group and down through the field,
spent the rest of the race buried in the midfield, and crossed the line
fifteenth, eight seconds behind the winner. The fastest lap of the race -
a 1:31.777 on lap 37, three tenths quicker than anyone else managed - reads
like a protest. Pole, fastest lap, zero points. It is only the second Sunday
this season he has left empty-handed, after his P16 at Barcelona, and the
first time it has happened from the front of the grid.

At the front, <DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> did what Ferrari have been
threatening since Barcelona - and then had to do it twice, because a safety
car erased every gap he had built and parked the field on his gearbox for
the run to the flag. He held them all off: <DriverChip ref="russell" />
0.427s back, <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> at 0.772s, <DriverChip ref="norris" />
at 1.149s, <DriverChip ref="hadjar" /> at 1.598s. Eight cars finished within
two and a half seconds of the winner - the closest top of the field 2026 has
produced - and none of them could find a way past a Ferrari that had been
second-best all day on Saturday.

The home crowd got a podium, if not the win - and it stings, because it
should have been second. <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> was running ahead of
Russell when the safety car came out, pitted, and gave the place away: he
rejoined behind the sister Mercedes and crossed the line third, 0.345s from
the position the stop had cost him. A home podium in Ferrari red all the
same, and another 15 points in his pursuit of the man who profited.
<DriverChip ref="russell" /> was the biggest winner of the
day he didn't win: second place and 18 points while his team-mate scored
nothing cuts the championship gap from 43 to 25. <DriverChip ref="norris" />
took fourth from sixth on the grid, best of a McLaren pair that split badly -
<DriverChip ref="piastri" /> went from eighth to eleventh and left his home
race with nothing.

Behind the leaders the RB team had their best Sunday of the season:
<DriverChip ref="lawson" /> sixth from tenth, second-season
<DriverChip ref="arvid_lindblad" /> seventh from ninth, fourteen points in one
afternoon. <DriverChip ref="bortoleto" /> took eighth for Audi, and Alpine
scored with both cars - <DriverChip ref="colapinto" /> driving from nineteenth
on the grid to ninth, <DriverChip ref="gasly" /> tenth.

<DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> never got that far. Seventh on the grid,
he retired with six laps to go and was classified twentieth - another Sunday
that promised more than it paid on a weekend where the sister Red Bull of
Hadjar finished fifth. <DriverChip ref="sainz" />, <DriverChip ref="alonso" />
and <DriverChip ref="stroll" /> came home a lap down.

<DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> still leads on 179 - every one of those points
scored before Sunday's race - but the shape of the table has changed. Russell's
second place brings him to 154, just 25 behind - the smallest the lead has
been since the start of May - and <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> is seven
further back on 147. The 25 points for the win lift <DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> from
the midfield knot to fourth outright on 108, clear of Norris (97), Piastri
(82) and Verstappen (76).

In the constructors' fight Ferrari's 40-point Sunday against Mercedes' 18 cuts
the gap from 100 to 78 - still a wall, but a lower one, and Ferrari have
outscored Mercedes 79-76 across the last three rounds. Further back, RB's
double score pulls them to 59, a single point behind Alpine for fifth.

[Spa](/races/2026/10/) is next on 19 July, two weeks out. Antonelli has led
the championship since March and still holds a race-win-sized cushion, but for
the first time all year the cars behind him are the ones with momentum - and
the Ardennes has a habit of punishing teams whose weekends have started coming
apart on Sundays.
