Four races into the new-regulations 2026 season and the championship table
reads in a way nobody had on their bingo card. [Andrea Kimi
Antonelli](/drivers/antonelli/) — in his second full F1 season, still 19 years
old — leads the standings on 93 points after winning three of the four rounds.
[Mercedes](/teams/mercedes/) sit first and second in both championships.
[Max Verstappen](/drivers/max_verstappen/) is seventh.

## What the table says

After [Miami](/races/2026/4/), the top of the drivers' championship is:

1. **Antonelli** — 93 pts, 3 wins
2. **[George Russell](/drivers/russell/)** — 67 pts, 1 win
3. **[Charles Leclerc](/drivers/leclerc/)** — 46 pts
4. **[Lewis Hamilton](/drivers/hamilton/)** — 43 pts
5. **[Lando Norris](/drivers/norris/)** — 38 pts
6. **[Oscar Piastri](/drivers/piastri/)** — 33 pts
7. **Verstappen** — 22 pts

Mercedes are on 160 points. [Ferrari](/teams/ferrari/) sit second with Leclerc
and Hamilton both consistently in the top six. [McLaren](/teams/mclaren/) — the
team that walked the second half of 2025 — have been the surprise underperformer:
their car is fast in qualifying but the new active-aero package looks edgier in
race trim than Mercedes' equivalent.

## How the four rounds played out

**[Australia (R1)](/races/2026/1/)** was Russell's. Pole, lights-to-flag,
fastest lap to Verstappen — who started P20 after a power-unit issue in
qualifying and dragged the Red Bull to sixth, the most Verstappen-coded recovery
in a season where almost nothing else has gone right for him. Antonelli was P2
from second on the grid.

**[China (R2)](/races/2026/2/)** flipped the Mercedes pecking order. Antonelli
took pole, led every lap and added the fastest lap. Russell finished second.
The other story was [Liam Lawson](/drivers/lawson/) outqualifying both Red
Bulls on a track where Verstappen has historically been untouchable.

**[Japan (R3)](/races/2026/3/)** was a Mercedes 1-2 lockout from pole, and the
first hint that Antonelli's pace at the front of a race is sustained rather
than a one-off. He's been the fastest car on long-run race pace at three of the
four weekends.

**[Miami (R4)](/races/2026/4/)** was the same script — pole, fastest lap, win —
but with Verstappen at least back on the lead lap in sixth. Ferrari's Leclerc
took third, his third podium of the year.

## Mercedes 1-2: the first time since 2020

A Mercedes team on 160 points after four races is something the sport hasn't
seen since the height of the hybrid era. The W17 is — by the eye test and the
sector data — the most consistent car in any condition: cool quali in
Melbourne, hot race in Miami, the medium-temp middle ground in Shanghai and
Suzuka.

The technical story behind it is its own post; the short version is that the
new 50/50 ICE-electric split favours teams who can package the bigger battery
and the new active-aero rear wing without compromising rear-end stability.
Mercedes appear to have nailed both.

## What to watch from here

- **Antonelli vs Russell**. Teammate fights at the front of a championship are
  the only thing that can derail a season this dominant. Russell is the
  experienced one but Antonelli currently has the pace.
- **Can Red Bull recover?** Verstappen's 22 points are fewer than he's scored
  by round four of any season since 2021. The RB22 looks like a midfield car
  in race trim; the Honda-era PU advantage is gone and the new active-aero
  package needs work.
- **McLaren's race pace**. Norris and Piastri are quick on Saturday and average
  on Sunday. With nine sprint weekends still to come, McLaren can't afford to
  keep losing 8-10 points to the leaders every race.

The 2025 title fight went to the final round. The way 2026 has started, it
might be over by the summer break.
