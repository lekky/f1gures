Four rounds in, three wins for <DriverChip ref="antonelli" />, who took pole
in [Miami](/races/2026/4/), led lights-to-flag and finished 3.264s clear of
<DriverChip ref="norris" />. A different second place this time - the McLaren
upgrade that's been promised for two months finally turned up - and a Sunday
that almost reset the championship narrative. Almost.

## The race

Antonelli converted from pole again, but Miami was the first weekend where
the front wasn't a Mercedes 1-2: <DriverChip ref="russell" /> qualified fifth
after a Q3 mistake at the stadium chicane, and the Saturday sprint hadn't
been kind either - he started Sunday fifth on the grid and finished fourth.
The story behind Antonelli was <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />, who
qualified an eye-catching second and led the opening stint when Antonelli
pitted, only for the Red Bull's race pace to fade once the tyres came back.
He finished fifth.

McLaren took fastest lap (Norris, 1:31.869 on lap 35) and the bigger half of
the podium. <DriverChip ref="piastri" /> took third from P7. Two podiums in
two weekends - five rounds ago this team had finished a combined P19 in
Shanghai and looked nothing like the testing favourites the paddock had them
as. Miami is the inflection point or it's a one-off; the next round will tell.

<DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> had a difficult day. P3 on the grid, P8 at the
flag - Ferrari haven't yet had a weekend in 2026 where both cars finished
ahead of both McLarens, and the trend lines crossing matters. <DriverChip ref="hamilton" />
took P6, his fifth top-six finish from four races - the consistency he was
hired for, even if the headline result hasn't come.
