The W17 is the fastest car in F1 in qualifying, in race trim, on slow circuits,
on fast ones. <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> took pole at [Suzuka](/races/2026/3/)
by 0.241s from <DriverChip ref="russell" />, took the fastest lap on lap 49,
and won lights-to-flag. Two wins from three rounds in his second season.

## The race

Antonelli converted pole cleanly, opened a 4-second gap by lap 10 and
controlled the race from there. The surprise was behind him: <DriverChip ref="piastri" />,
starting third, undercut Russell during the first round of stops and held
him out for the entire second stint to take P2. McLaren's first podium of
the year, and a result that probably keeps <DriverChip ref="piastri" />
sane after two ugly weekends.

<DriverChip ref="leclerc" /> finished third from fourth on the grid - Ferrari's
race-pace deficit to Mercedes is real but smaller here than it was in
Shanghai. <DriverChip ref="russell" /> recovered from the undercut loss to
P4, his worst finish of the season. <DriverChip ref="norris" /> took P5 and
his first points of 2026 - a result that gets less embarrassing the further
McLaren is from where the testing data suggested they would be.

## The Verstappen question

P8, two laps down on Antonelli when the chequer dropped. <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" />
qualified P11. The RB22 had a clean weekend - no PU issues, no incidents -
and the car was eighth-fastest on race pace. Helmut Marko's post-race comments
mentioned the word *struggling* four times in the same paragraph. Red Bull
are in trouble, and it isn't temporary.

## The DNFs

- <DriverChip ref="stroll" /> - lap 14, hydraulics again, the third Aston DNF
  in three rounds
- <DriverChip ref="bearman" /> - lap 36, contact with <DriverChip ref="albon" />
  at Spoon Curve - one of Haas's first rounds without a finish since the
  start of last season
