A new regulations era, a new fastest car, an old name on top. <DriverChip ref="russell" />
took pole on Saturday, led every lap on Sunday and crossed the line in [Melbourne](/races/2026/1/)
three seconds clear of his teammate. Mercedes look exactly as quick as the
testing whispers suggested.

## How it unfolded

Russell converted from pole cleanly - <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> got close
into Turn 1 but didn't have the line. From there the Mercedes pair pulled away
on the medium and never gave the rest of the field a sniff. <DriverChip ref="leclerc" />
held a quiet third for the entire race; <DriverChip ref="hamilton" /> recovered
from seventh on the grid to fourth, the kind of debut Ferrari result that
counts as a relief rather than a celebration.

The real story was further back. <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> had a PU
issue in Q1 and started P20. Red Bull put him on a long opening stint and a
fresh hard, and he drove from twentieth to sixth, taking the fastest lap on
lap 43 in the process. It is the most Verstappen-coded performance you can
imagine in a year that is otherwise shaping up to be a Mercedes coronation.

<DriverChip ref="norris" /> finished fifth, the only McLaren in the points -
<DriverChip ref="piastri" /> qualified fifth, lost the rear at Turn 6 on lap
one, and never recovered, crossing the line P21 a lap down. Not the McLaren
result anyone in Woking had on the bingo card.

## The DNFs

- <DriverChip ref="stroll" /> - lap 14, suspension failure after kerb damage
- <DriverChip ref="alonso" /> - lap 26, brake-by-wire fault
- <DriverChip ref="bottas" /> - lap 31, electrical (Sauber)
- <DriverChip ref="hadjar" /> - lap 38, retired from a strong P3 grid spot
  with a sensor issue. The rookie weekend of the year, ruined by the car.

## What it means

One race, but a clean read on the pecking order: Mercedes ahead, Ferrari
second, McLaren a project, Red Bull a midfield car in race trim with one
driver still capable of dragging it somewhere it doesn't belong. [China](/races/2026/2/)
in a week is a sprint weekend - first chance to see whether Saturday-Sunday
specialism shifts the picture.
