The 2026 season comes to [Île Notre-Dame](/circuits/villeneuve/) on Sunday
May 24 - the fifth round, the second sprint weekend in a row, and the first
race in a time zone the European broadcast booth doesn't have to apologise
for. <DriverChip ref="antonelli" /> arrives with three wins and a 26-point
lead. <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> arrives seventh in the
championship on 22 points, the worst start to a season he's had since 2020.

## What Canada asks of the new cars

Three demands the previous four circuits haven't really made of the 2026
package:

- **Traction out of slow corners.** The Circuit Gilles Villeneuve is a
  stop-start layout punctuated by three hairpins and the slow chicane onto
  the pit straight. Cars that recover energy efficiently into the corner and
  deploy it cleanly on exit gain laptime that won't show up on lower-grip
  circuits. With 50/50 PUs and active-aero, this is the first race that
  properly stress-tests the regulations' core idea.
- **Kerb tolerance.** The chicanes are aggressive. Cars that ride them get
  the laptime; cars that don't either lift or break.
- **The Wall.** The final chicane onto the pit straight has a concrete
  barrier on the exit that has put more world champions (Schumacher,
  Villeneuve, Hill, all on the same Sunday in 1999) into the wall than any
  other inanimate object in F1. With unfamiliar 2026 cars, an active-aero
  system that drops downforce on the straight, and weather that historically
  reaches in and changes the weekend without warning, expect a hit list.

## The storylines

- **Can anyone stop Antonelli?** The W17 has had race-pace advantage at every
  weekend so far. Canada's lower-downforce setup window is the first track
  that might neutralise some of it. If Mercedes win here too, the title
  conversation moves from *when* to *which Mercedes*.
- **The Russell counter.** Five years older, the racecraft is on his side,
  and Antonelli has yet to face a teammate fight at the front of a real
  championship. Russell's Montreal record - two podiums, one win in 2024 -
  is one of the strongest in the current field.
- **Red Bull's Canada upgrade.** A new floor and revised cooling are the
  rumoured Miami-spec package that <DriverChip ref="max_verstappen" /> said
  *didn't really do what we wanted*. Canada has another iteration of it. Red
  Bull cannot afford another P5-best weekend.
- **McLaren consolidating.** Two podiums in two rounds for <DriverChip ref="piastri" />
  and <DriverChip ref="norris" /> respectively. A third weekend on the
  podium with both cars would close the gap to Ferrari in the constructors
  to under 20 points.
- **The weather.** Forecasts on Thursday show a 60% chance of rain on Sunday.
  Montreal in wet conditions is a genuinely different race; the safety car
  appears once for every two visits across the last decade.
