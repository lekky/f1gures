Qualifying is the Saturday shootout that sets Sunday's grid. It is short, frantic
and decided by single laps, and once you know its three-part shape you can read
the timing screens at a glance.

## Three knockout parts

Qualifying runs in three segments. **Q1** drops the slowest five cars, **Q2**
drops five more, and the remaining ten fight for pole in **Q3**. Wherever a driver
is knocked out is where they start, so a Q1 exit means a back-of-grid slot for
Sunday.

## A track that speeds up

As more cars run, they lay down rubber that grips better, so the surface gets
faster through the session. This is **track evolution**, and it is why drivers
often wait to run their fastest lap as late as possible, when the track is at its
best.

## The tow

Running close behind another car on a straight gives a **tow**, a slipstream that
adds top speed. On power circuits a well-timed tow can be worth a few tenths, so
teams sometimes coordinate which car runs first.

> A scruffy out-lap can ruin everything. Tyres have a narrow temperature window,
> so drivers weave and brake hard on the way round to have the rubber switched on
> for that one flying lap.
