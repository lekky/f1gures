Track limits sound like a technicality until a deleted lap costs a pole or a
podium. The rule is simple: stay inside the white lines.

## The white line rule

The track is defined by the solid **white lines** at its edges. A car has left the
track if all four wheels go beyond the white line. Run wide there and any lap time
you set is **deleted**, which matters enormously in qualifying.

## Gaining an advantage

In the race, leaving the track and coming back faster, or completing a pass off
track, counts as **gaining an advantage**. The usual fix is to hand the position
back; ignore it and the stewards step in with a [penalty](/guide/penalties/).

## Strikes add up

Most circuits use a tally. Cross the limit too many times, often three warnings,
and the next offence brings a penalty. It keeps drivers honest at corners where
running wide is tempting and fast.
