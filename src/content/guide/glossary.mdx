Commentary moves fast and assumes you know the lingo. Keep this list handy and the
jargon stops getting in the way.

## On track

The language of racecraft and the racing surface:

- **Dirty air** — Turbulent wake behind a car that costs the one chasing grip in corners.
- **Slipstream** — The pocket of low drag behind a car that lets a follower carry more speed.
- **Marbles** — Bits of worn rubber off the racing line; slippery to drive on.
- **Apex** — The point a driver clips on the inside of a corner.
- **Backmarker** — A slower car about to be lapped by the leaders.

## Strategy and tyres

The words that decide races off the track:

- **Undercut** — Pitting before a rival to use fresh-tyre pace and jump ahead.
- **Overcut** — Staying out longer than a rival to gain on older tyres in clear air.
- **Stint** — A run of laps on one set of tyres, between stops.
- **Deg** — Short for degradation, the loss of tyre grip over a stint.
- **The cliff** — The sudden point where a worn tyre stops working.

## Car and session

Hardware and weekend terms in brief:

- **Power unit** — The full hybrid engine package, not just the V6.
- **Parc fermé** — The state from qualifying on where set-up is locked.
- **Formation lap** — The warm-up lap before the start that sets the grid.
- **Out-lap** — A lap leaving the pits, used to warm tyres and brakes.
- **Pole position** — First on the grid, earned by the fastest qualifying lap.
