The start can make or break a race in a few seconds. Twenty-two cars launch together
into the first corner, and the order at the end of lap one often looks nothing
like the grid.

## Formation lap

Before the race the field completes a **formation lap** to warm tyres and brakes,
then reassembles on the grid in qualifying order. Anyone who cannot hold position
or stalls must start from the back or the pit lane.

## Lights out

Five red lights come on one by one, and the race begins the instant they all go
out. The gap before they extinguish is deliberately variable, so no driver can
time it; they react. A clean getaway is about clutch control and traction off the
line.

## Jumping the start

Sensors watch each car. Move before the lights go out and the stewards issue a
penalty, usually five seconds. The first braking zone into turn one is the biggest
overtaking chance of the race, when cars are bunched and tyres are cold (see
[overtaking](/guide/overtaking/)).
