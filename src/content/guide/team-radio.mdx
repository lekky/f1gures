Radio messages are clipped and full of jargon on purpose. A handful of phrases
unlock most of what you hear on a Sunday.

## Why it sounds like code

Radio time is short and the car is loud, so teams compress everything into set
phrases. Once you know a handful, the broadcast snippets suddenly make sense.

## The common calls

These are the ones you will hear most weekends:

- **Box, box** — Come into the pits this lap. "Box" comes from the painted pit box.
- **Push** — Drive flat out now, stop managing the car.
- **Lift and coast** — Ease off before braking zones to save fuel or cool the brakes.
- **Plan B** — Switch to the pre-agreed alternative strategy.
- **Delta** — A target time to run to, often under a virtual safety car.
- **Hammer time** — Famous shorthand for a few all-out qualifying-pace laps.

## Reading the tone

Calls are kept calm on purpose. A driver who starts talking a lot is often
managing a problem; a quiet car is usually a happy one. The best clues to a race's
story are in what the radio does not say. For the wider vocabulary, see the
[jargon glossary](/guide/glossary/).
