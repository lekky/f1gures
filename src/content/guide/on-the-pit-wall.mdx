Behind every driver is a crew making split-second calls. Knowing who does what
turns the radio chatter into a story you can follow.

## The race engineer

Every driver has one voice in their ear: the **race engineer**. They relay
strategy, warn about rivals and traffic, manage the car's settings and keep the
driver calm. The relationship is one of the closest in the sport, built over years.

## The strategists

Back in the garage and at the factory, a **strategy team** runs constant
simulations: when to stop, which tyre to fit, how a safety car would change
things. They turn live data into the calls the race engineer passes on.

- **Race Engineer** — The driver's single point of contact on the radio.
- **Strategist** — Models pit windows and tyre choices in real time.
- **Performance Engineers** — Tune the car's systems, brakes and power unit.
- **Team Principal** — Runs the operation and owns the big calls.

## Who decides

In the heat of a race the strategists recommend and the driver has a say, but the
final word on a borderline call usually sits with the **team principal** or
sporting director on the pit wall. Good teams make those decisions in seconds.
