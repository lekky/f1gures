F1GURES Driver page · heat-map mockups

Three ways to bring the Baseball-Savant "heat" language onto a driver page. Each ranks Lando Norris against a population and paints the result on a cold→hot scale. All use existing f1gures tokens (surfaces, type, mono numerics, hard corners, team colour on a strip) plus one new --heat-* scale.

Idea 1

Percentile Rankings

The faithful adaptation. Every metric is plotted on a 0–100 percentile vs the current grid, fill + marker coloured by heat, raw value on the right. Grouped into skill blocks with a team-colour rule. Best as a headline panel right under the hero.

Idea 2

Career Heat-Grid

Same heat language, totally different surface. Every race of the driver's career as one cell — rows are seasons, columns are rounds — coloured by finishing position (P1 hot red → backmarker cold blue, DNF crossed). A whole career's form readable in one glance, like a GitHub contribution graph.

Idea 3

Skill Heat-Dials

The hero-friendly take. A row of radial gauges — one per composite skill — with the arc swept and coloured by heat. Big, dramatic, scannable; pairs with the percentile panel (dials = summary, bars = detail) or stands alone as a compact "scouting" strip.
